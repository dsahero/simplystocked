About the Market of Virginia Tech
Our Story
The Market of Virginia Tech supports food access for our community in ways that respect the diverse needs and dignity of those served.

The Market of Virginia Tech is an initiative of Student Affairs designed to provide food assistance to students who, for whatever reason, have a hard time obtaining regular, healthy meals. This situation could be the result of a short-term disruption in finances, residing in a food desert, or a lack of access to other financial assistance.

When Hema and Mehul Sanghani learned how many Virginia Tech students were struggling to have enough healthy and nutritious meals, they felt compelled to act. Hema Sanghani is a member of the Virginia Tech Class of 1999 who earned her degree in finance, and a Virginia Tech Foundation board member. Mehul Sanghani is a member of the Virginia Tech Class of 1998 who earned bachelor’s degrees in industrial and systems engineering and psychology. He is CEO and founder of Octo Consulting Group, a nationally recognized technology and consulting firm based in Reston, Virginia. Mehul Sanghani is a native of Blacksburg, home of Virginia Tech’s largest campus. Hema Sanghani grew up in Lynchburg, Virginia, a less than two-hour drive from that campus.

Supported by a $1.5 million donation from the alumni couple, the university launched The Market of Virginia Tech to enhance food access for Hokies.

The program is run by Virginia Tech Student Affairs, which encourages any undergraduate or graduate students who faces food insecurity to utilize one of the many program offerings provided by The Market of Virginia Tech.

Food Security Programs
Primary Programs
Currently we offer two primary programs to support student food access: Open Market Hours and The Market’s Grocery Store Model. Each program is designed to support the various types of needs students may have, be it food type, length of support needed, and location.

To determine which program might best suite your current needs please contact the Dean of Students office.

Open Market Hours
Open Market Hours are times that any Virginia Tech student can drop in at The Market and access our non-perishable pantry. No sign up, forms, or screening required!

Location: 801 University City Blvd., Blacksburg, VA, 24060

Grocery Store Model
The Grocery Store Model is The Market of Virginia Tech’s landmark model. Students enrolled in this program can shop for a variety of produce, dairy, dairy substitutes, protein, and non-perishable items each week. This program is limited in capacity due to financial and space limitations – we can only currently enroll 115 students for 30-weeks of support. If you are a student interested in enrolling in this program, please complete the intake form.

Due to our current waitlist size, it is unlikely that those who join the waitlist will receive support within 6 months of completing the form. Please refer to Open Market Hours (no intake form necessary), other food access resources in the NRV, and the Dean of Students office for additional support.

Frequently Asked Questions
I have an emergency need for food support, where do I go?

Please reach out to the Dean of Students office at dean.students@vt.edu or 540-231-3086.
Look into our Open Market Hours to see what times might work for you. There is no pre-registration or application, simply walk in when open. You must be a Virginia Tech student to utilize this resource.
I am not a Virginia Tech student; can I utilize The Market of Virginia Tech’s programs?

No. Currently The Market of Virginia Tech can only support current Virginia Tech students. For all our programs, you must be a current, enrolled, student or enrolled for classes for the following semester if you are accessing during summer or winter breaks.
For community resource options, please find more information on the NRV Food Access Directory.
My organization wants to do a food drive for The Market, how do we start?

Please visit our Giving page to learn more about what types of food we need most, how to go about collecting food, and how to arrange a drop-off time for your donation drive.
I want to support The Market of Virginia Tech, but I don’t know how, where do I start?

Thank you for your interest in supporting our work! Please visit our Giving page to learn more about avenues for support.
I want to donate something to The Market, but I’m unsure if it is allowed, what should I do?

Thank you for your interest in giving to The Market of Virginia Tech! Please visit our give page to learn more about what types of donations we accept. If you cannot find an answer, reach out to us at themarket@vt.edu and we can assist you further.
I am not a United States Citizen, can I still utilize your services?

Yes, The Market of Virginia Tech’s services are confidential and will not be reported to any governing body. Please feel free to utilize any of our programs as needed. If you have any further questions or concerns, please contact us at themarket@vt.edu.
How do I know if I qualify for The Market’s services?

There is no screening for any of The Market’s services. Unfortunately, the enrolled grocery story model does require an application as we have set capacity. This program is first-come first serve, so you may not be enrolled simply because of space – you will be added to the waitlist. Otherwise, you are free to utilize any of our programs – no qualification necessary.
I have been on the waitlist for the grocery-store model for a long time, does that mean I am not eligible for the grocery-store model or that I should re-apply?

No, there is no screening for this model. You are likely still on the waitlist due to our capacity being full. You do not need to reapply as our waitlist carries over, you will simply move up as space is available. We will reach out to you once you are able to enter the program. In the meantime, please utilize our Open Market Hours to find support. Or reach out to the Dean of Students office for additional information.
Someone put a food drive box in an area that is not allowed, what can The Market do?

The Market of Virginia Tech does not currently have any facilitated food drives on campus. Outside groups, organizations, etc. can and do host food drives on their own, these are not the responsibility of The Market. We cannot help if a box is placed in an unacceptable location and recommend you simply remove the box or determine who the owner of the box is.
